Who Can Donate Blood?

  • Age between 18-65 years
  • Weight at least 50kg (110 lbs)
  • Good general health
  • Hemoglobin level of at least 12.5g/dl
  • Normal blood pressure (120/80)
  • No major surgeries in the last 6 months
  • No current medications (some exceptions apply)

Who Cannot Donate Blood?

  • People with HIV, Hepatitis B, or Hepatitis C
  • Those who have had a heart attack or stroke
  • Pregnant women
  • People with diabetes requiring insulin
  • Cancer patients
  • Those who have received a tattoo or piercing in the last 4 months
  • People who have traveled to malaria-risk areas recently

After Blood Donation

Recovery Time: Your body will replace the fluid lost from donation within 24 hours.
  • Rest for at least 15 minutes after donation
  • Drink extra fluids for the next 24 hours
  • Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous exercise for 24 hours
  • Keep the bandage on for at least 4-5 hours
  • Wait at least 56 days before donating whole blood again
  • Eat iron-rich foods to help replenish your blood

Blood Donation Awareness

  • One unit of blood can save up to three lives
  • Blood cannot be manufactured; it can only come from donors
  • Less than 10% of eligible population donates blood
  • Blood is needed every two seconds
  • All blood types are needed, especially O negative (universal donor)
  • Platelets are especially needed by cancer patients
  • Blood donation is a safe process with sterile equipment
Did you know? World Blood Donor Day is celebrated on June 14th every year!
